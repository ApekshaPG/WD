7 Wonders of India

A visual journey through India's most iconic landmarks

1. Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum in Agra, built by Shah Jahan in memory of Mumtaz Mahal. A UNESCO World Heritage Site and symbol of India's rich history.

2. Khajuraho Temples

Built between 950–1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty, these temples in Madhya Pradesh are famed for their intricate carvings and erotic sculptures.

3. Hampi

Once the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire (1336–1565), Hampi in Karnataka is a sprawling ruin of temples, palaces, and bazaars set amidst giant boulders.

4. Golden Temple

The holiest gurdwara of Sikhism in Amritsar, covered in gold leaf and surrounded by a reflective pool, offering peace and communal kitchens feeding thousands daily.

5. Qutub Minar

Started by Qutb-ud-Din Aibak in 1199 and completed by Iltutmish, this 73m minaret in Delhi is a masterpiece of early Indo-Islamic architecture.

6. Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple in Odisha, shaped like a gigantic chariot with twelve pairs of stone wheels, dedicated to the Sun God Surya, dates to the 13th century.

7. Ajanta & Ellora Caves

These rock-cut cave complexes near Aurangabad, Maharashtra, span 600 years of art and architecture, showcasing Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain traditions.
