Seven Wonders of India

1. Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum in Agra, India.
Built by Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.
It's considered one of the most beautiful buildings in the world.
It is a symbol of India's rich history.
It was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, to house the tomb of his favourite wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

2. Khajuraho Temples

The Khajuraho temples are a group of temples in the small village of Khajuraho in Madhya Pradesh, India.
The temples were built between 950 and 1050 AD by the Chandela dynasty.
They are known for their intricate sculptures and are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

3. Hampi

Hampi is a town in the Vijayanagara district of Karnataka, India.
It was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire from 1336 to 1565.

4. Golden Temple

The Golden Temple is spiritually the most significant shrine in Sikhism.
It became a centre of the Singh Sabha Movement between 1883 and the 1920s,
and the Punjabi Suba movement between 1947 and 1966.

5. Qutub Minar

Qutbu'd-Din Aibak laid the foundation of Minar in AD 1199 for the use of the mu'azzin (crier) to give calls for prayer.
The first storey was raised by him, and three more storeys were added by his successor and son-in-law, Shamsu'd-Din Iltutmish.

6. Sun Temple

A sun temple is a building dedicated to the sun or a solar deity.
People perform religious and spiritual activities there.
Sun temples are found in many cultures around the world, including India, China, Egypt, Japan, and Peru.

7. Ajanta and Ellora Caves

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves are a group of rock-cut cave temples and monasteries in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India.
They are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and are known for their art and architecture.
